01
The insight
The Bay Area has one of the highest concentrations of wellness-conscious, high-income adults in the country — and most of them have already tried IV therapy. They love it. They just can't get it reliably, affordably, or from someone they actually trust. The big IV lounges are clinical and impersonal. The pop-up services are inconsistent. Nobody has built the version of this that feels like calling your NP best friend.
02
The gap
Right now, if you want quality IV hydration in Oakland or SF, you either book a stranger through a Medspa app, overpay at a retail lounge, or go without. What's missing is a trusted, licensed, relationship-based provider who comes to you — and who you can actually text. That relationship doesn't exist at scale yet. We're building it.
03
The approach
Nurses Call The Shots is a mobile IV hydration and concierge wellness service built on two things: Coco's clinical credibility as a licensed NP, and a membership model that creates predictable recurring revenue. We come to you — home, office, pop-up event — and we build a real client relationship, not a transaction. Kelly runs operations, partnerships, and growth. Coco runs the clinical side. The business is built to operate lean and hit $10K/month within 90 days of launch.
04
The proof
Coco's existing Oakland network already uses IV therapy. These aren't cold leads — they're warm referrals from people who know her, trust her clinically, and have asked when she's launching. We have a validated demand signal before spending a dollar on marketing. The model — 45 IVs/month at $225 average — is the same math that's working for solo NP practices across every major metro right now.
05
The ask
We're raising $100K from 4–6 investors — people who already know the value of what we're building because they've lived it. Your investment covers six months of operating runway, legal infrastructure, clinical setup, and launch marketing. In return: equity in the company, $0.80 on every dollar invested returned as service credits, and first access as we expand into GLP-1, NAD, and concierge care. At month six, we'll be at $10K+/month and positioned to scale.
Delivery note: This pitch is meant to be spoken, not emailed. The warm network version skips the formal intro and opens with: "You know me, you know Coco, and you've probably already asked when she's launching something — here's what we're building."

01
Lead with the relationship, not the business
Every angel in Coco's network already trusts her clinically. The pitch is "Coco is launching and I want you to be part of it" — not a cold investor deck. Open conversations personally before anything formal is sent.
02
Lead with credits, follow with equity
$16K in IV service credits is tangible and personal to someone who already gets IVs. Equity in a pre-revenue startup is abstract. Sequence matters: credits → first access → equity.
03
Never say "we could hit $10K/month" — say "the model requires 45 IVs"
Specific mechanics are more credible than projections. Show the math. 45 IVs at $225 average is 11 per week. That's 2–3 per day. That's a real thing a real NP can do.
04
Name the risk before they ask
The honest note for every angel: "The biggest risk is the 30–45 day legal setup window before we earn dollar one. We've budgeted for that. It's the reason we're raising 6 months of runway, not 4."
05
The angels ARE the first clients
Structure credit redemption so the first invitations to book are sent to investors. They become testimonials, referral sources, and proof-of-demand simultaneously. Make this explicit in the pitch.
