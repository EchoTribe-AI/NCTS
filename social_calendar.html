"Your labs look fine." Why that sentence has stopped helping you.
The most validating educational post of the calendar. Explains the gap between standard lab ranges and functional wellness — without bashing doctors.
"Your labs look fine." If you've heard this sentence and still felt like something was wrong — you weren't imagining it. Here's what's actually happening: Standard lab reference ranges are designed to identify disease. They're calibrated to catch what's pathological — not to optimize how you feel. So when your TSH, B12, or vitamin D comes back "in range," what that often means is: you're not sick enough to flag. That's not the same as feeling well. Functional wellness asks a different question. Not "what's wrong with you?" but "what does your body need to actually thrive?" A lot of women I see are living in the gap between "nothing is technically wrong" and "I genuinely don't feel like myself." That gap is real. It has causes. And there's a lot we can do about it. As an NP, my scope of practice lets me look at your health differently than a GP running a standard panel. I'm not here to diagnose — I'm here to listen, look at the full picture, and help you feel like yourself again. That's what a real provider relationship looks like. Founding member waitlist is open. Practical Tip
5 signs your body is depleted — not just stressed.
Free clinical value that builds trust before the ask. The highest-save post type. Bridges from "I'm tired" to "I need support."
Stress and depletion feel similar from the inside. But they have different causes — and different solutions. Here's how I tell them apart clinically: 1️⃣ Sleep doesn't restore you. Stress makes it hard to fall asleep. Depletion means you sleep 8 hours and still wake up exhausted. If rest isn't restoring you, your body is missing something — not just wound up. 2️⃣ You've lost tolerance for things that didn't used to bother you. Loud environments, a packed schedule, even social plans that should feel fun. Depleted nervous systems have less buffer. Everything costs more than it used to. 3️⃣ Your appetite has changed — but not in an obvious way. Not necessarily less hungry. More like disconnected from hunger cues. Reaching for food out of habit or need for stimulation rather than actual hunger. 4️⃣ You feel better after an IV, a really good meal, or a rest day — but it doesn't last. Your body is telling you exactly what it needs. It just can't hold onto it. 5️⃣ You're doing "everything right" and still feel like something's off. The supplements, the sleep hygiene, the green smoothie. When the inputs are right and the output is still wrong — that's a delivery problem, not a discipline problem. Sound familiar? Save this and share it with someone who needs to see it. We can help. Link in bio. 🌿

NP vs. RN vs. MD. Why it matters who's actually at your door.
The credential explainer — accessible, not defensive. Builds confidence in Coco's clinical scope and differentiates NCTS from RN-only competitors.
Let's talk credentials — because in mobile wellness, who's showing up actually matters. Here's the plain-language breakdown: 🩺 RN (Registered Nurse) Trained to administer treatments and provide direct patient care. Skilled, essential, and the backbone of healthcare. Most mobile IV services send RNs. They execute the protocol — they don't write it. 🩺 MD (Medical Doctor) 8+ years of training, diagnostic authority, prescribing rights. Rarely does house calls. When they do, the overhead is significant and the warmth is often... clinical. 🩺 NP (Nurse Practitioner) Master's level clinical training. Independent prescribing rights in California. Diagnostic scope. And — critically — trained in a nursing philosophy that centers the whole person, not just the presenting complaint. An NP can assess, diagnose, prescribe, adjust, and follow up. Not as a workaround. As a fully licensed independent provider. When Coco shows up at your door, she's not executing a protocol someone else wrote for you. She's your provider. She knows your chart. She can pivot if something doesn't feel right. That's the difference. Founding members get ongoing NP care — not just a drip. Link in bio. 🌿
